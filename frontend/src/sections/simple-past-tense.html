Simple past tense expresses actions or events that occurred in the past.

In past simple, the verbs being used have 2 forms; namely regular verbs and irregular verbs.

The regular form of past simple is formed by adding '-ed' to the end of the root or infinitive verb (e.g., bake becomes baked). On the other hand, the irregular form of past tense includes changing the infinitive verb (e.g., swim becomes swam) or even no change at all (e.g., cut is still cut).

Forming simple past tense

Here are some examples of forming simple past tense using regular and irregular verbs:

Affirmative

  • I read a book at the library yesterday.
  • She forgot to attend the lecture this morning.
  • The shop offered a good discount on some chocolate products.
  • The cat deliciously ate the chicken that we prepared last night.
  • He was the top student back in college

Negative

In simple past tense, we can form negative sentences by adding 'not' after the auxiliary verbs. The verb that follows the auxiliary verb (e.g., was, were, and did) or modal auxiliary verbs (e.g., could, might, would, and should) still remains in the base form.

For example:

  • I did not work yesterday as I was sick.
  • The coffee shop did not sell your favourite milkshake yesterday
  • Andy was not in the soccer team last season.
  • I could not make it to the wedding on time.
  • The lecturer did not come to class today.

Interrogative

  • Did you come to school yesterday?
  • Did she not call you before coming to the office?
  • Did the HR discuss your salary in yesterday's meeting?
