The simple present tense in English has few interpretations which depends on the context of the usage (Cowper, 1998).

Sartika (2020) defines present tense with four main uses, including a) repeated or regular actions in the present or habitual activity; b) reveal facts; c) express things that are always true.

Cowper (1998) also provided some examples of the various usages of simple present tense, including:

  1. Brigitta takes the train to work everyday. (habitual activities)
  2. Kelvin has short hair. (displays facts)
  3. That restaurant on the corner of the street sells a good pasta. (things that are always true)
  4. Dog has four legs. (displays facts)

How to apply simple present tense

In the simple present tense, the root form of the verb is used, but an s or es is added to the verb when it is in the third-person singular form.

The grammatical sentences can be formed by applying the formula below:

I/You/We/They + verb + object

Example:

  • I go to university every two days a week.
  • You eat that bread deliciously.
  • We normally drive to the lesson.
  • They do not work in this building.

He/She/It + verb(s/es) + object

Example:

  • He goes to school by bike every morning.
  • She normally opens the shop around 7.30 am in the morning.
  • Look at that puppy over there, it sleeps on the sofa every evening.

Forming grammatical sentences

Affirmative

  • I like to eat banana bread.
  • She likes to study English.
  • Brigitta goes to work early in the morning.

Negative

  • I do not like to eat banana bread.
  • She does not work late today because of assignment.
  • Brigitta does not floss her teeth sometimes.

Interrogative

  • Do I like banana bread?
  • Does Kelvin own a laptop?
  • Does she not go to dance lesson every Saturday morning?
