Future perfect tense is used to describe action that will happen, scheduled to happen, and planned to occur. Unlike simple present tense or past tense, simple future tense adds an auxiliary verb 'will' before the infinitive verb.

The formula is subject + will + infinitive verb, example as follows:

  • I will attend the lecture at 10 am tomorrow!
  • The postman will deliver our parcels soon.
  • Mickey will not buy the phone because it is too expensive.
  • Will you bake some cakes today?

Unlike simple present and past tense where we have to conjugate the infinitive verb, simple future tense does not require verb conjugation. The root verb will remain the same regardless of the subject (I/You/(He/She/It)).

Forming simple future tense

The declarative sentences of simple future tense state that something will happen or fact that some events will occur, including prediction and expectation in the future.

Affirmative

  • The school event will be attended by the local mayor.
  • She will come to the office a bit late due to the bad weather.
  • I will bring the blanket for the sleepover tonight.
  • The bank will announce the cash rate next month
  • The shop will close a bit late tonight at 9 pm due to late night shopping

Negative

  • I will not attend the lecture today.
  • She will not come late today as today is her special day.
  • Andy will not buy the laptop because of the price.
  • The event will not be a success without your contribution.
  • He will not tell you where he is going.

Interrogative

  • Will she eat the prawns if she is allergic?
  • Where will you go for this upcoming school holiday?
  • Will you call me once you are in the theatre?
  • Will Andy pass the math exam?
  • What will you do when you get there?
